Manager · Operations Guide

Approvals, oversight & reporting

As a manager you are the gate-keeper of stock data quality and the second pair of eyes on every Purchase Order, Sales Order and clerk submission. This guide walks you through your daily review routine and the levers you have to keep the warehouse running cleanly.

1Your daily routine

A 15-minute morning ritual that catches 90% of issues before they grow.

Open dashboard→ Clear pending approvals→ Review POs & SOs in flight→ Scan exceptions→ Brief the team
ApprovalsClerk-submitted item creations and edits waiting for your sign-off.
Purchase OrdersDrafts to approve, deliveries to receive, partials to chase.
Sales OrdersDrafts to confirm, shipments in progress, cancellations.
ReportsKPIs, low-stock list, inventory value, movement trends.
Customers / SuppliersMaster records of trading partners.
AuditingEvery action a clerk took, fully traceable.
ℹ︎
Your job in one sentence You don't enter movements yourself — you make sure the people who do, do it correctly, on time, and against the right paper trail.

2Reading the dashboard

Four widgets answer the question "is anything on fire today?".

  1. KPI strip — Active SKUs, Inventory value, Low-stock count, Out-of-stock count, and Issued in the last 30 days. Any number flashing red means it crossed a threshold overnight.
  2. Stock health chart — daily issued vs received curve. A widening gap (more out than in) means you'll run dry soon for those items.
  3. Pending approvals tile — shows the count of clerk submissions waiting for you. Click through to clear them.
  4. Recent activity — the last few transactions, newest first. Use this to spot a clerk who's stuck or who entered something unusual.
✓
Tip Switch the dashboard's date filter to Yesterday first thing in the morning — it shows you what closed out overnight and what didn't.

3Approving clerk submissions

Clerks can't add or change items directly — every change waits in your queue.

Open Approvals from the sidebar. The page lists every pending request with the clerk's name, the action they tried (CREATE / UPDATE / SOFT_DELETE) and a short summary.

  1. Click a row to expand it. The Item / summary column shows the proposed values; for an UPDATE it shows old → new for each changed field.
  2. Sanity-check:
    • Does the SKU look like the rest of the catalogue? (e.g. FLR-50KG-NF, not flour bag.)
    • Are Category and Unit of measure sensible?
    • Is Reorder point set so we won't get caught short?
  3. Tick the request(s) you're happy with using the leftmost checkbox.
  4. Press Approve selected. You'll be asked for your PIN — this is your audit signature, do not share it.
  5. Wrong submission? Tick it and press Reject selected. Always type a short reason — the clerk sees it on their dashboard.
⚠
Don't approve in bulk without reading. A bad SKU created today is a stock-take headache forever. Take the 10 seconds per row.
ℹ︎
Approved changes go live immediately. Once you approve, the item appears in every Movements / PO / SO / Kits picker. There is no second sign-off.

4Purchase Order workflow

The five PO statuses and what to do at each one.

DRAFTClerk created it. You review and submit.
SUBMITTEDSubmitted to you. You approve.
APPROVEDSent to supplier. Wait for delivery.
PARTIALSome lines received, some still owed.
CLOSEDAll received (or you closed it short).

Approving a draft PO

  1. Open Purchase Orders and click the row of the PO with status SUBMITTED.
  2. On the right-hand Workflow (selected row) card, press Submit if the clerk forgot to (status moves DRAFT → SUBMITTED), then press Approve.
  3. The PO is now visible to the supplier and ready to receive.

When a delivery arrives

The clerk will normally do this. Your job is to spot mismatches:

  • Status stuck in PARTIAL for more than 7 days → chase the supplier or close it short.
  • Receive quantity entered as zero on every line → the clerk forgot to enter the qty before pressing Receive line. Reject and ask them to redo.
  • Cost variance against the PO (rare) → record it via a manual Stock In adjustment with a clear reference, and note it for the supplier reconciliation.
✕
Closing a PO short is a deliberate decision. Once you press Close, the missing balance disappears from "expected stock". Make sure it's recorded as a supplier shortfall in your manual log.

5Sales Order workflow

From customer order to fully shipped, with you confirming at the right moments.

DRAFTClerk built it. You confirm to commit stock.
CONFIRMEDStock is reserved. Clerk picks & ships.
PICKINGSome lines shipped, some still owed.
SHIPPEDAll lines fully shipped.
CANCELLEDYou voided it; no stock moved.
  1. Open Sales Orders. The right-hand Workflow card lists every order.
  2. Pick a DRAFT from the dropdown. The lines table shows what's on it, totals at the bottom.
  3. Check the customer, the items, and the prices match the customer's PO / quote.
  4. Press Confirm — the order is now CONFIRMED and the clerk can ship.
  5. If something's wrong, press Cancel order instead. Cancellation does not consume stock.
⚠
Cancelling a partially shipped SO is not possible. Once a line has been shipped, the order can only be closed naturally. If you really need to undo a shipment, contact the admin — they can post a reversing entry.

6Reading reports

The numbers managers care about, and what to do when they shift.

The five KPI cards

KPIWhat it meansWatch for
Active SKUsHow many products are in the catalogue.Sudden jumps suggest duplicate items being created — check Approvals.
Inventory value (cost)Stock quantity × unit cost across the warehouse.A drop without matching sales = stock leaving without a receipt.
Low-stock SKUsItems below their reorder point.Action: raise a PO before the count rises further.
Out-of-stock SKUsItems with quantity ≤ 0.If a shipped SKU shows OOS, you may be over-issuing — investigate.
Issued (30d)Total issued in the last 30 days, with net change.Negative net = you're consuming faster than receiving.

Charts & tables below the KPIs

  • Daily flow chart — the two-line graph (received vs issued) tells you if you're trending toward a stock-out.
  • Top movers — the SKUs with the most activity. Often where errors hide.
  • Aging stock — items sitting too long. Talk to sales about discounting.
✓
Export anything you need. The grids on Reports / Movements / Inventory all have a column-header → Export CSV option. Use it for board packs and for sharing with finance.

7Customer & supplier maintenance

Keep the partner records current so POs and SOs route to the right people.

Adding a customer

  1. Open Customers.
  2. Press + New customer.
  3. Enter Customer code (short unique code, e.g. CUST-JBA-001) and Name.
  4. Add contact phone, email, and address. These appear on every SO printed for this customer.
  5. Press Save.

Adding a supplier

Same flow on the Suppliers page. The Lead time (days) field on a supplier is used by the dashboard to forecast when a re-order should be placed.

ℹ︎
Don't delete partners with history. Use the Inactive toggle instead so old POs/SOs still resolve their partner name correctly.

8Handling clerk errors & exceptions

The most common things that go wrong, and the fastest way to fix them.

SymptomLikely causeYour fix
PO stuck in PARTIAL for daysSupplier didn't deliver the rest.Press Close on the PO; log the shortfall.
SO can't ship — "qty would exceed remaining"Clerk over-counted on a previous shipment.Open Movements, post a Stock In correction with reference Reverse over-ship of SO-XXXX, then ship.
Item negative on ReportsStock Out posted before the matching Stock In.Find the missing receipt in Auditing and post it via Stock In with the original delivery note as reference.
Duplicate SKU appearedTwo clerks submitted the same item from different shifts.Approve one, reject the other with reason "duplicate of SKU-XYZ".
Approval queue piling upYou're the bottleneck.Either approve daily or delegate the role to a second manager via the Users page.
✕
Never edit the database directly. Every correction must go through the UI so the audit trail captures it. If you find yourself wanting to "just fix it in SQL", call the admin.

9Weekly & monthly checks

A short ritual that keeps the warehouse honest over time.

Every Friday

  • Approvals page is empty.
  • No PO is older than 14 days in SUBMITTED or PARTIAL.
  • No SO is older than 7 days in CONFIRMED without a single shipment.
  • Low-stock list reviewed; POs raised for the top 5.

Monthly

  • Run a cycle count on a sample of SKUs (rotate so every SKU is counted at least once a quarter).
  • Compare physical count to system value; for any variance > 2%, post a corrective Stock In/Out with reference CYCLE-COUNT-YYYY-MM.
  • Export the inventory value report and send it to finance.

10Best practices for managers

Five habits that keep your team running and your reports trustworthy.

  1. Clear approvals daily. A queue older than 24 hours starts to slow the warehouse.
  2. Always reject with a reason. Train the team without having to repeat yourself in person.
  3. Confirm Sales Orders the same hour they arrive. Reserved stock = honest "available to promise" figures.
  4. Keep your PIN secret. Approvals are signed with your PIN; if you share it, you sign their work.
  5. Use Auditing, not memory. When something looks off, the Auditing page tells you who, what, when — don't ask, look.
