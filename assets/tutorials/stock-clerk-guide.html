Stock Clerk · Operations Guide

How to register entries in the inventory system

Every box that arrives, every item that leaves the shelf, and every move between bins must be recorded so the stock figures stay accurate. This guide walks you through each task in plain language, with the exact fields you'll see on screen.

1Your daily workflow

A quick map of what you'll be doing across the system.

As a stock clerk you are the system's eyes and ears on the warehouse floor. The most common entries you will register, in roughly this order during a typical day, are:

Receive deliveries → Put away into bins → Pick & ship orders → Record corrections → Reconcile at end of day
Movements Quick stock in / stock out without a paper PO or SO. Used for ad-hoc deliveries, returns to shelf, samples, breakages.
Purchase Orders Receive ordered goods against a supplier PO. Stock comes in line by line.
Sales Orders Pick and ship goods against a customer order, line by line.
Locations Create new bins and move quantity from one bin to another inside the same warehouse.
Inventory Look up an item's master data, register new SKUs (sent to your manager for approval).
Kits & BOM Assemble finished products from their components.
ℹ︎
Golden rule Record the entry the moment the physical movement happens. If you wait, the on-screen quantity will not match what's on the shelf and pickers downstream will be misled.

2Logging in & navigating

Get in, get oriented, get to work.

  1. Open the system in your browser at the URL given by your supervisor (for example https://inventory.shop.com.ss).
  2. On the welcome page click Sign In, then enter your username and password.
  3. Choose your preferred language at the top right (English / العربية / Juba Arabic / Dinka). Your choice is remembered next time.
  4. Use the left sidebar to jump between modules. The pages you'll use most are Movements, Purchase Orders, Sales Orders and Locations.
✓
Tip The little ? next to a page title gives a one-line reminder of what the page is for. Hover or tap it any time you need a refresher.

3Recording a delivery (Stock In)

Use this when goods arrive without a formal Purchase Order — for example, a small ad‑hoc delivery, a customer return going back on the shelf, or a sample.

Open Movements from the sidebar. The leftmost card is titled Stock in.

FieldRequired?What to enter
Which item? Required Type a few letters from the item name or its SKU (e.g. Maize) and pick from the list.
How many? Required The quantity in the item's normal unit of measure (bags, cartons, pieces…). Decimals are allowed for items measured by weight.
Cost per unit (if known) Optional If the supplier invoice quotes a cost per unit, enter it here so the inventory value stays accurate. Leave blank if you only know the total or it's a return.
Delivery note or reference Optional The delivery note number, supplier waybill or anything that lets the auditor trace the entry back to a paper document.
Receive into location Optional The bin you're placing the goods in. If left blank, the system uses the item's default storage location.
  1. Fill the fields above as completely as you can.
  2. Press Save stock in.
  3. A green confirmation message appears at the top — your entry is saved.
  4. The history table at the bottom of the page now includes the new transaction with type STOCK_IN.
⚠
If the goods arrived against a Purchase Order, do NOT use this page. Use Receiving a Purchase Order instead, otherwise the PO will stay open and the supplier balance will be wrong.

4Recording an issue (Stock Out)

Use this when stock leaves the shelf without a formal Sales Order — internal use, a damaged item written off, samples, etc.

Still on Movements, use the middle card titled Stock out.

FieldRequired?What to enter
Which item? Required Search and pick the item leaving the shelf.
How many? Required The quantity going out. Must not exceed what's on the shelf for that item.
Who / what for? Optional The reason — staff name, department, "damaged", "sample to client X", etc. This is what the auditor will read first.
Issue from location Optional The specific bin you took it from. Leave blank if it doesn't matter.
  1. Fill in the fields.
  2. Press Save stock out.
  3. A confirmation appears and the history table updates with a STOCK_OUT entry.
ℹ︎
The system always uses the oldest stock first (so older deliveries clear the shelf before newer ones). You don't have to choose which delivery batch to draw from — just record the quantity.

5Receiving a Purchase Order

When the goods on the truck match a PO that was raised earlier, receive them line by line so the PO is closed properly.

  1. Open Purchase Orders from the sidebar.
  2. In the table at the bottom, find the PO you're receiving against (status will usually be APPROVED or PARTIAL) and click its row to select it.
  3. On the right-hand Workflow (selected row) card, type the quantity actually received for the line into Receive qty (line).
  4. Press Receive line.
  5. Repeat for every line on the delivery note.
  6. When every line is fully received, press Close to mark the PO complete.
⚠
Quantity mismatch? If the supplier delivered fewer items than ordered, only enter what actually arrived. The PO stays open for the missing balance — it does not auto-cancel. Tell your manager so they can chase the supplier.
✕
Never receive more than ordered. If the supplier sent extra, set the rest aside and inform your manager — the surplus must be added through a separate Stock in with a clear reference, not buried inside a PO line.

6Transferring stock between bins

When you physically move stock from one bin or aisle to another, register the move so the bin map stays correct.

Open Locations from the sidebar and use the right-hand Transfer between bins card.

FieldRequired?What to enter
Item Required Search and pick the item being moved.
From Required The bin you're taking the stock out of.
To Required The bin you're placing the stock into.
Quantity Required How much you're moving.
Reference Optional Any short note explaining why (e.g. "consolidating broken pallet", "moved closer to dispatch door").

Press Transfer. The grid below refreshes to show the new per-bin balances.

Need a brand-new bin first?

  1. On the same page, use the left card New storage location.
  2. Enter the Bin name (e.g. A-12).
  3. Enter the Warehouse (defaults to Main).
  4. Optionally enter a Zone (e.g. COLD for refrigerated).
  5. Press Create location. The new bin is immediately available in every From / To picker.

7Picking & shipping a Sales Order

When a customer order is ready to leave the warehouse, ship it line by line.

  1. Open Sales Orders from the sidebar.
  2. In the right-hand Workflow card, pick the order from the Select order dropdown — orders with status CONFIRMED or PICKING can be shipped.
  3. The status badge under the dropdown turns to the colour of the current state. The line table at the bottom now shows the order's lines, with the columns Ordered, Shipped and Price (SSP) right-aligned and a Total row at the bottom.
  4. Under Ship line, choose the line to ship from Order line (the dropdown only lists lines with quantity still to ship).
  5. Enter Qty to ship. It must not exceed what's still owed on that line.
  6. Optionally pick the From location (the bin you took it from).
  7. Press Ship. The grid refreshes and the line's Shipped column ticks up.
  8. Repeat for every line until the order is fully shipped.
✓
Partial shipments are normal. Ship what you actually have — the rest stays open and you can ship it on a later run. Do not invent a shipment to "close" a line you can't fulfil.

8Registering a new item (SKU)

When the supplier brings a product the system has never seen before, you create the master record. Your changes go to your manager for approval before they go live.

  1. Open Inventory from the sidebar.
  2. Press + New item at the top of the page.
  3. Fill the form. The fields you must always set are:
    • Item ID / SKU — a unique short code (e.g. FLR-50KG-NF).
    • Name — the human-readable name (e.g. Wheat Flour 50kg).
    • Category and Unit of measure — pick from the dropdowns.
  4. Helpful but optional:
    • Reorder point — the system will warn when stock falls below this number.
    • Reorder quantity — how much to order when restocking.
    • Default storage location — saves you a click later.
    • Supplier, Barcode, Expiry date if applicable.
    • Unit cost and Unit price — used to value stock and to pre-fill prices on Sales Orders.
  5. Press Save.
ℹ︎
Your save goes into a queue. You'll see a confirmation that the request was sent to a manager for approval. Once they approve it on the Approvals page, the new SKU shows up everywhere (Movements, POs, SOs, Kits…). Until then it is not visible in those pickers.

9Assembling a kit

When you physically combine components into a finished product (a hamper, a bundle, a relief kit), record the assembly so the components are subtracted and the finished item is added.

  1. Open Kits & BOM from the sidebar.
  2. On the left card, pick the Finished product. The components table below shows what each unit of the finished product is made of.
  3. On the right card Assemble kits, enter Quantity to build (e.g. 20 for twenty hampers).
  4. Optionally choose Receive into location for where the finished kits will sit.
  5. Press Assemble.

The system removes the right amount of each component from stock and adds the finished products. If any component is short, the assembly fails with a clear message — top up the missing component and try again.

⚠
Don't assemble what you haven't physically built. The point of this entry is to mirror reality — record the assembly only after the kits are actually packed and on the shelf.

10Checking history & corrections

How to find a past entry and what to do if you made a mistake.

Finding a past entry

  1. Open Movements.
  2. On the right card History, optionally pick a single item under One item only to filter.
  3. Press Refresh table.
  4. The grid at the bottom shows up to 500 recent transactions, newest first. Each row has the type (STOCK_IN, STOCK_OUT, PO_RECEIPT, SO_SHIPMENT, TRANSFER, ASSEMBLE…), quantity and reference.
  5. Use the column header filter to search by reference or transaction ID.

I made a mistake — what now?

✕
Do not delete or hide the wrong entry. Every entry stays in the audit log. The correct fix is to post a compensating entry that reverses it.
  • Recorded too much Stock in? Post a Stock out for the difference, with a reference like Correction of TX-XXXXX.
  • Recorded too much Stock out? Post a Stock in for the difference, same kind of reference.
  • Wrong PO line? Tell your manager — they can re-open the line on the Approvals page.
  • Wrong shipment on a Sales Order? Tell your manager — only ADMIN/MANAGER can reverse a shipment.

11Daily best practices

Five habits that keep your numbers trustworthy.

  1. Record first, then move. Enter the transaction, get the green confirmation, then carry the goods.
  2. Always add a reference. Future you (and the auditor) will thank you. Even a few words like "broken in transit" is enough.
  3. Match the paper. If there's a delivery note or invoice number, copy it into the reference field exactly.
  4. Don't leave drafts hanging. Confirm or cancel draft Sales Orders / POs by end of day so the dashboard reflects reality.
  5. End-of-day check. Open the dashboard and scroll the "Recent activity" widget — every physical move you did today should be there. If something is missing, post it before logging out.
✓
If in doubt, ask. A 30-second WhatsApp to your manager beats an hour of stock-take reconciliation next week.
